# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Mixed audience: potential clients/employers evaluating Zach's skills and fit, collaborators and peers looking for a creative partner, and curious visitors discovering his work. They arrive wanting to understand *who Zach is* and *what he makes* — the work should speak clearly without requiring effort.

### Brand Personality
**Curious, inventive, warm.** Zach is a Marine Engineer and Entrepreneur who builds real things — hardware, boats, hydrofoils, electronics. The portfolio should feel like the person behind the work: hands-on but thoughtful, technically rigorous but not cold, creative without being pretentious.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Editorial/magazine feel** — typographic, layout-driven, closer to a printed monograph than a typical web portfolio. Think independent design press: considered type hierarchy, varied image sizes, text and image in dialogue. Not a gallery app. Not another Adobe Portfolio clone.

Anti-references: generic SaaS dashboards, glowing dark-mode tech portfolios, identical card grids, developer-tool aesthetics (monospace, terminal vibes).

Light mode preferred. Warmth in the palette — slightly warm whites, not pure #fff.

### Design Principles
1. **Type carries meaning** — typography should do real work, not just label things. Hierarchy through size, weight, and spacing.
2. **Variety over uniformity** — not every project needs the same card. Editorial layouts break the grid intentionally.
3. **Work first, chrome second** — the projects are the content; navigation and structure should recede.
4. **Warmth in the details** — color, font choice, and spacing should feel human and considered, not generated.
5. **Titles are not secrets** — project names and context should be legible at rest, not hidden behind hover states.
